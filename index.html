About Eldorado Park

Eldorado Park was developed into a residential area by the then Department of Community Development primarily to accommodate members of the colored race in Terms of the Group Areas Act and to resettle families of color displaced from area declared for whites only.

Initially the administration of housing estates in Eldorado Park Proper, Ext 1, 3 and 8 was vested in the authority of Community Development and when Ext 2, 4, 6 and 7 was developed through funds advanced by the National Housing Commission, these properties were devolved to the City of Johannesburg areas further developed by the City through loans were Ext 9, 10 infill scheme and more recently the Delft Scheme.

The properties consist of various typologies ranging from Economic, Lower and Sub-economic freestanding houses, semi-detached houses totalling approximately 16000 units and 1000 row houses, the approximately 4000 flats range from bachelor, 1-bedroom, 2-bedroom and 3-bedroom duplex. In addition to this the area is further populated by families in occupation of backyard shacks, resulting in an estimated population of 250 000 persons.

The Unemployment level in the area is estimated at 35%, the majority being the youth income levels ranges from low to medium with very few households earning high income. The primary level of education is matric with very few being able to provide unto tertiary level due to affordability. The high level of unemployment has also impacted directly towards increasingly high levels of crime and substance abuse.

COVID-19
Coronavirus Disease
Updated : 24/03/2020
Overview

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a new version of the Corona virus. The disease causes respiratory illness (like the flu) with symptoms such as a cough, fever, and in more severe cases, difficulty breathing.

You can protect yourself by washing your hands frequently, avoiding touching your face, and avoiding close contact (1 meter or 3 feet) with people who are unwell. How it spreads Coronavirus disease spreads primarily through contact with an infected person when they cough or sneeze.

It also spreads when a person touches a surface or object that has the virus on it, then touches their eyes, nose, or mouth.

Prevention

Help stop coronavirus with these steps

1. HANDS - Wash them often
2. ELBOW - Cough into it
3. FACE - Don't touch it
4. SPACE - Keep safe distance
5. HOME - Stay if you can
6. MASKS - For populated areas as a precaution

Theres currently no vaccine to prevent coronavirus disease (COVID-19). You can protect yourself and help prevent spreading the virus to others if you: Do Wash your hands regularly for 20 seconds, with soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub Cover your nose and mouth with a disposable tissue or flexed elbow when you cough or sneeze.

Avoid close contact (1 meter or 3 feet) with people who are unwell Stay home and self-isolate from others in the household if you feel unwell Don't Touch your eyes, nose, or mouth if your hands are not clean

Symptoms

People may be sick with the virus for 1 to 14 days before developing symptoms. The most common symptoms of coronavirus disease (COVID-19) are fever, tiredness, and dry cough.

Most people (about 80%) recover from the disease without needing special treatment. More rarely, the disease can be serious and even fatal.

Older people, and people with other medical conditions (such as asthma, diabetes, or heart disease), may be more vulnerable to becoming severely ill. People may experience: cough fever tiredness difficulty breathing (severe cases)

Treatment

There is no specific medicine to prevent or treat coronavirus disease (COVID-19). People may need supportive care to help them breathe.

If you have mild symptoms, stay at home until you have recovered. You can relieve your symptoms if you: rest and sleep keep warm drink plenty of liquids use a room humidifier or take a hot shower to help ease a sore throat and cough.

If you develop a fever, cough, and have difficulty breathing, promptly seek medical care. Call in advance and tell your health provider of any recent travel or recent contact with travelers.
